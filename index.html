Understanding Mental Health

Mental health is vital for overall well-being. Learn about common mental health disorders and recognize early signs through self-assessment tests. Awareness and understanding can help reduce stigma and encourage timely support.

Depression

General Info

Depression is a common mental health condition characterized by persistent sadness, low energy, and loss of interest in daily activities. It affects thoughts, feelings, and behaviors, often leading to social withdrawal, reduced productivity, and difficulty coping with stress.

Early recognition and intervention improve recovery. Support from family, therapy, medications, and healthy lifestyle changes can make a significant difference.

Symptoms

  • Persistent sadness or emptiness
  • Loss of interest in hobbies
  • Changes in sleep or appetite
  • Fatigue or low energy
  • Feelings of guilt or worthlessness
  • Difficulty concentrating

Anxiety

General Info

Anxiety is a natural response to stress, but excessive or persistent worry that disrupts daily life may indicate an anxiety disorder. It can affect physical health, work, and relationships.

Managing anxiety involves recognizing triggers, practicing relaxation techniques, maintaining social support, and seeking professional therapy when needed.

Symptoms

  • Excessive worrying
  • Panic attacks
  • Restlessness or agitation
  • Rapid heartbeat or sweating
  • Difficulty concentrating
  • Trouble sleeping

Bipolar Disorder

General Info

Bipolar disorder is a mental health condition involving extreme mood swings from manic highs to depressive lows. These mood changes can disrupt daily life, work, and relationships.

Early support, structured routines, therapy, and sometimes medications help stabilize mood and improve functioning.

Symptoms

  • Manic: high energy, little sleep, impulsive actions
  • Depressive: sadness, fatigue, loss of interest, hopelessness
  • Irritability or agitation
  • Difficulty concentrating during mood episodes

ADHD

General Info

ADHD (Attention-Deficit/Hyperactivity Disorder) affects focus, attention, and impulse control. It can impact academic performance, work, and social relationships.

Recognizing symptoms early and implementing behavioral strategies, therapy, and routine management can help manage daily challenges effectively.

Symptoms

  • Difficulty focusing or paying attention
  • Forgetfulness and disorganization
  • Impulsive behavior
  • Restlessness or hyperactivity
  • Difficulty completing tasks

PTSD

General Info

PTSD (Post-Traumatic Stress Disorder) may develop after experiencing or witnessing traumatic events. Symptoms can persist long-term and interfere with daily life.

Early therapy, support networks, and coping strategies are crucial for recovery and reducing distressing symptoms.

Symptoms

  • Flashbacks or nightmares
  • Avoidance of trauma reminders
  • Hypervigilance or heightened startle response
  • Emotional numbness
  • Difficulty concentrating or sleeping

Eating Disorders

General Info

Eating disorders involve unhealthy attitudes or behaviors related to food, weight, and body image. They can seriously impact physical and mental health.

Early awareness, therapy, nutrition counseling, and supportive environments improve recovery and prevent complications.

Symptoms

  • Anorexia: extreme restriction of food intake
  • Bulimia: bingeing and purging behaviors
  • Binge Eating Disorder: uncontrolled eating episodes
  • Preoccupation with body image or weight
  • Emotional distress around food
